About Me

I am an IT professional with extensive experience in computer networking, cybersecurity, systems administration, software and web development. I like to tell people that I never feel like I go in to work because I do what I love every day. I can honestly say that there are few things in this world as great as living each day doing something that you're passionate about and I feel blessed knowing that's exactly the outlook I have on the career I've chosen.

You could say that my career in IT began as a kid. Like many others I started playing video games and that’s a passion that I’ve never lost. In fact, it was the reason I chose to create this Discord-inspired portfolio. Gaming has defined me as a person, and I expect that’s a fair assessment of many individuals in this industry. Sometime around my 15th birthday, my dad and I took a used computer I had bought apart. I remember going to Fry’s electronics and buying some replacement thermal paste and a new hard drive and additional RAM. I believe this is what really sparked my interest in IT but I didn’t realize it then.

It would take several more years and a few blue-collar jobs to realize that I wanted to work with my brains and not my brawns. I have a huge respect for people who work physically demanding jobs. But there’s a certain enjoyment I get from a real mental challenge. And so, I went back to school. I really wanted to get into software development. I did a few programming courses (primarily C#) but at this point in my life, I also didn’t want to spend 3 more years earning a degree in software development. So, I fast tracked a degree in cybersecurity in a shorter manner of time by also taking CompTIA and Microsoft training courses at night for certifications and college credit. In short time, I started working as an IT Systems Technician for the training center I was attending. After graduation I took a job as a Help Desk Technician for a medical organization in San Jose.

Eventually my wife was offered a position in our home state of Indiana and I decided it was time to return to Indiana. I took a job as a systems technician for a Managed Service Provider. I loved the work and got to meet a lot of wonderful people in the area. I continued earning certifications and it wasn’t long before I started getting offers for systems administrator and network administrator positions. I ended up taking a position as a network administrator for a sales company, where I’m still at today.

As time has passed, I’ve noticed that even in my capacity as a network administrator, I’m always looking for ways to write scripts and automate tasks. In short time, I started researching software development bootcamps. I came across Eleven Fifty Academy, and that is where I’m at now. I’m currently finishing my gold badge while working fulltime. I love infrastructure, cybersecurity, networking, and IT service management. But I especially enjoy creating software to solve problems. It’s my hope to eventually move into a DevOps position and utilize my refined skills in software development alongside network administration skills and experience.
